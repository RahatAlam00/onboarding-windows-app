\# Occupational Health \& Safety (OHS) – Laptop Ergonomics



\## Risks of Using a Laptop Without an External Monitor or Keyboard



Working directly on a laptop for long periods can lead to:



\* Neck pain from looking down at the screen.

\* Shoulder and upper back strain.

\* Wrist discomfort from poor keyboard positioning.

\* Poor posture and slouching.

\* Eye strain from prolonged screen use.

\* Increased risk of repetitive strain injuries over time.



\---



\# Ergonomic Equipment That Can Improve Posture



Some equipment that can improve comfort and reduce strain includes:



\* A laptop stand to raise the screen to eye level.

\* An external keyboard.

\* An external mouse.

\* An adjustable ergonomic chair.

\* An external monitor for larger displays.

\* A footrest if the chair height cannot be adjusted comfortably.



\---



\# Healthy Workspace Setup



To improve ergonomics:



\* Position the top of the monitor at approximately eye level.

\* Sit with feet flat on the floor.

\* Keep elbows close to the body at roughly a 90-degree angle.

\* Keep wrists in a neutral position while typing.

\* Sit back against the chair with proper lumbar support.

\* Position the monitor about an arm's length away.



\---



\# Daily Habits to Reduce Strain



Helpful habits include:



\* Taking short movement breaks every 30–60 minutes.

\* Stretching the neck, shoulders, wrists, and back.

\* Following the 20-20-20 rule to reduce eye strain (every 20 minutes, look at something about 20 feet away for 20 seconds).

\* Checking posture regularly.

\* Alternating between sitting and standing when possible.
