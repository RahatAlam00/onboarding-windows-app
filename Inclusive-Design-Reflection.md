\# Inclusive Design Reflection



\## Who Are Considered Vulnerable Populations?



Vulnerable populations include groups who may face additional barriers when accessing products, services, or workplaces. Examples include:



\* People with ADHD

\* People with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

\* People with physical or cognitive disabilities

\* Elderly individuals

\* People experiencing mental health challenges

\* Individuals with low digital literacy



In digital spaces, these users may face challenges such as information overload, confusing navigation, sensory overload, inaccessible content, and difficulty maintaining focus or completing tasks.



\---



\## Ethical Considerations When Designing for Neurodivergent Individuals



When designing for neurodivergent users, it is important to:



\* Respect different ways of thinking and working.

\* Avoid overwhelming interfaces and excessive cognitive load.

\* Use clear, predictable navigation.

\* Provide flexibility so users can customize their experience.

\* Avoid manipulative or shame-based design patterns.

\* Ensure accessibility and inclusivity are considered throughout development.



The goal should be to support users rather than force them into a specific way of working.



\---



\## Making Interactions and Content More Accessible



To make products more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism:



\* Use simple and clear language.

\* Break information into smaller sections.

\* Maintain consistent layouts and navigation.

\* Reduce unnecessary visual clutter.

\* Provide clear instructions and expectations.

\* Allow customization of notifications, colours, and focus settings.

\* Use predictable workflows and terminology.



Reducing cognitive load helps users focus on their goals rather than figuring out how the interface works.



\---



\## Supporting Neurodivergent Team Members



In a professional setting, we can support neurodivergent colleagues by:



\* Communicating clearly and directly.

\* Providing written instructions when possible.

\* Respecting different communication styles.

\* Allowing flexibility in how work is completed.

\* Giving constructive and specific feedback.

\* Avoiding assumptions about how people prefer to work.

\* Creating an environment where questions are encouraged.

\## What Are Some Common UX or Communication Pitfalls?



Some common pitfalls include:



\* Overly complex interfaces.

\* Too much information presented at once.

\* Inconsistent navigation.

\* Unclear instructions or expectations.

\* Excessive notifications and distractions.

\* Using technical jargon without explanation.

\* Assuming all users learn and process information in the same way.



These issues can increase frustration and make products less accessible.

\# Task



\## First-Person Account Reviewed



I watched a first-person video from an individual with ADHD discussing the productivity tools that have helped them stay consistent and productive. One point that stood out to me was that many popular productivity tools become overwhelming for people with ADHD because they contain too many features, too many lists, or require too much planning and maintenance. The creator emphasized that ADHD-friendly tools should be simple, flexible, and easy to use without creating additional cognitive load.



The creator explained that they prefer systems that help them quickly capture ideas, separate important tasks from less important ones, and provide enough structure without feeling restrictive. They also highlighted that people with ADHD often need more flexibility than traditional productivity systems provide. For example, rigid time-blocking schedules often failed for them, whereas larger time windows and more freedom within a schedule worked much better.



Another important takeaway was that many ADHD challenges are related to task initiation and overwhelm rather than a lack of motivation. The creator described using tools that reduce friction, break tasks into smaller steps, and help build momentum instead of relying on willpower alone.



After watching this account, I better understood the importance of reducing cognitive load, simplifying workflows, and providing clear guidance when designing productivity software. It reinforced how important it is to create tools that support users rather than overwhelm them with complexity.

\## One Design Improvement For Focus Bear



One improvement would be to provide more contextual explanations for features such as Focus Alignment Score, TOP Score, and focus modes.



As a new user, I found some of these concepts difficult to understand immediately. Providing tooltips, examples, or quick explanations directly within the interface could reduce confusion and improve accessibility.
